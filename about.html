ABOUT

LET'S EAT

The Tiki Terrace was founded in April of 2003 by brothers, Scott, Jim and Phil Zuziak. It was their intention to bring Hawaii to the Chicago-land area through the sights, tastes and sounds of Polynesia. All three brothers have a background in Polynesian drum & dance and have performed and competed in Samoan fire knife dancing competitions. The Tiki Terrace is a result of their passion for Polynesian culture all while reflecting their background with hand crafted décor and Hawaiian style local cooking. We have had the honor of being featured on many television shows, the latest being Chicago's Best! Please watch the WGN featured video below to get a taste of what a visit to The Tiki Terrace is like.

SIGHTS

Enjoy you meal and forget you're in Chicago as your submersed in lahala thatching, outrigger canoes, tiki tapa lamps, palm trees and hand carved tables. All of the décor is hand made by the Zuziak brothers and has been enjoyed by many, looking to escape over to Hawaii in Des Plaines! The sights continue on stage while Hula girls are dancing and taking you on a voyage throughout Polynesian. The atmosphere is tropical and relaxing no matter what season of weather we have in Chicago-

SOUNDS

The Tiki Terrace always has the favorite local music playing, both classics you'll recognize and some modern Hawaiian tunes. Don't forget to check out our EVENTS PAGE where we always have both live Hawaiian music and special concerts flown in from Hawaii! The Tiki Terrace has hosted names such as Jake Shimabakura, HAPA, Raiatea Helm, Stephen Espaniola, Abe Lagrimas, Kimo Hussey and much more! There's always a fun and exciting Hawaiian music event going on here at Tiki- Also we hold Hawaiian music night weekly featuring local artists such as "A Bunch of Coconuts", "The Wackey Keys", "The Tiki Cowboys, "Na Mele Aloha" and once a month Pesi Mauga singing karaoke-The Sounds of Hawaii are ongoing and daily at The Tiki Terrace-

TASTES

The Tiki Terrace proudly serves up a variety of local style dishes- There are also some unique dishes that are must tries! Weather you miss home or want to try something new, you'll enjoy the flavors of Hawaii- Please see our WEEKDAY MENU page and our WEEKEND SHOW MENU page.
